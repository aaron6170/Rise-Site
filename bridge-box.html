What's Inside

Every box is packed with intention.

Each item in the Recovery Bridge Box is carefully researched and chosen based on peer-reviewed evidence. Nothing is in here by accident. Every item is chosen to help someone take their very next step toward recovery.

📋 Research-backed • Stigma-free • ~$7 per box
💧

Water Bottle

Stay Hydrated

After an overdose, the body often needs hydration. Staying hydrated helps with mood, focus, and clarity, making it easier to engage with the other resources in this box.

🥑

Nutritional Snack

Something to Eat

It's common to have low energy after an overdose. A simple snack helps stabilize blood sugar, eases irritability, and makes it a little easier to think about next steps.

✍️

Handwritten Note of Encouragement

A Personal Touch

A short handwritten note from a real person makes a difference. It shows someone cares, and that simple reminder of compassion can be exactly what encourages someone to reach out for help.

📋

Rehab Resource Handout

Know Where to Go

Many people leave the hospital without a clear idea of what to do next. This handout lists local treatment options so the path forward is right there when it's needed most.

🧪

Fidget Object (Stress Ball)

Something to Hold

Having something to squeeze or fidget with can help ease anxiety and restlessness. It's a simple tool that gives your hands something to do when emotions feel overwhelming.

👓

Journal + Pencil

Write It Out

Sometimes writing things down helps. A journal and pencil give you a private space to sort through thoughts, process emotions, or just put words to what you're going through.

🩹

Blanket

Warmth and Comfort

Leaving the hospital can feel cold in more ways than one. A blanket offers physical warmth and a small sense of comfort during an incredibly hard moment.

📱

QR Code to R1SE

Scan for instant access to our website, clinic finder, and peer support resources. Help is just one scan away.

How It Works

From our hands to theirs.

1

We Build

Volunteers and staff assemble each box with care and intention.

2

We Deliver

Boxes are delivered to partner hospitals, ERs, and treatment centers.

3

They Receive

Staff give boxes to patients at the critical moment of discharge or stabilization.

4

They Rise

Armed with resources and hope, they take their first steps toward recovery.
